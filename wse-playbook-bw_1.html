Content Plan

6 Topics That Make
AI Recommend WSE

Every article, video, and post we create fits into one of these six topics. These are the exact topics that AI tools like Google, ChatGPT, and Perplexity are asked about most — and where we want WSE to be the answer.

Topic 1

How to Learn English

Helpful guides on grammar, speaking fluency, accent improvement, and business English. AI search loves clear, useful how-to content.

Topic 2

English for Your Career

Real data on how English affects salaries and promotions in Indonesia. Stories from professionals who advanced because of better English.

Topic 3

English in Indonesia

Research on English skills in Indonesian workplaces, industries that demand English most, and how Indonesia compares globally.

Topic 4

The WSE Method & Success Stories

Explaining how WSE teaches, and real stories from students who improved. This proves WSE works — which is what AI uses to verify a brand's credibility.

Topic 5

Comparing English Courses

"WSE vs other courses," "online vs offline," "group vs private classes." Comparison content is one of the most-searched topics — and AI loves to cite it.

Topic 6

English for Different Life Stages

Kids, teenagers, working professionals, executives, corporate teams. Separate content for each group means we show up for every type of search.

Must-Have

  • Write every article in a format that AI can easily read and quote
  • Make sure each article answers a specific question someone might type into Google
  • Include real data and local Indonesia context in every piece
  • Link articles to each other so AI sees WSE as a deep, trusted source

YouTube Channel

Must-Have

  • Always publish a full written version of every video on our blog

The APEX Framework
WSE's Marketing Operating System

Every campaign — big or small — must follow these four steps in order. Skip one, and the campaign will underperform. Follow all four, and WSE builds lasting visibility that compounds over time.

Think of APEX like building a house. You need the foundation first (A), then the walls (P), then make it livable (E), and finally grow it over time (X). Every campaign starts at A and works through to X.
1
Step 1 of 4 · Brand Foundation

A — Authority: Agree on What WSE Stands For

Before writing a single word or spending a single rupiah, every team member must agree on the same answers: Who is WSE for right now? What do we want people to feel when they think of WSE? What makes us different from other English courses? This step takes 30 minutes — but skipping it causes months of confusion.

🎯
Our Core Promise

Premium quality, real transformation, proven method, global credibility. This stays the same in every campaign.

💡
What We Offer Each Person

Practical English skills (functional). Confidence to speak up at work (emotional). Becoming someone who connects with the world (personal).

🧬
Our Personality

Expert but friendly. Global but Indonesia-aware. Premium but practical. Every piece of content must feel like this.

🏛️
Our Track Record

50+ years globally, proven results. This is what makes AI and humans trust us — and it must appear in our content.

📍
Who This Campaign Is For

Executives care about leadership. Professionals care about promotion. Students care about opportunity. Companies care about team ROI. Pick one focus.

🖼️
Look & Feel

All visuals, colours, and writing must match WSE's global brand guidelines. Consistency builds recognition.

Action
Before every campaign: hold a 30-minute alignment meeting. Write down the target audience, the one main message, and the brand personality check. All team members — brand, sales, content — must sign off before moving to Step 2.
2
Step 2 of 4 · Build Visibility

P — Presence: Make Sure People (and AI) Can Find Us

Now we create and publish content that helps people discover WSE — through our own website, through news media, and through AI search tools. The goal is simple: when someone searches anything related to English learning in Indonesia, WSE should appear.

📰
Our Own Content

8–12 articles per month. YouTube videos with written transcripts. LinkedIn articles. This is the base everything else builds on.

🗞️
Media Coverage

Features on Kompas, Detik, Glints, SWA. When trusted sites mention WSE, AI learns to trust WSE too.

🤖
Writing for AI

Every article must answer a specific question, include real numbers, and be structured so AI can easily read and quote it.

📊
Our Own Research

"WSE English in Indonesia Report 2026." Publishing our own data is the single most powerful thing we can do for AI visibility.

📣
News & Announcements

Monthly press releases sent to Antara, Detik, and wire services. Gets WSE into Google News and AI training data.

🗺️
Google Maps Profiles

Fully updated Google Business profile for every branch. AI uses this for "English course near me" searches.

Action
Every campaign needs: at least 1 article on our website + 1 feature in an external media + 1 press release. Before publishing anything, check: Does this answer a real question? Does it include data? Can an AI easily read and quote this?
3
Step 3 of 4 · Build Connection

E — Experience: Make People Feel Something About WSE

People have heard of WSE. Now they need to feel connected to it. This step is about creating real experiences — events, influencer stories, ads, and free tools — that turn awareness into genuine interest and trust.

🎬
Influencer Stories

YouTube reviews, LinkedIn articles, podcast episodes. At least 2 influencers per campaign, both required to publish written content online.

🎪
Events & Workshops

The Glints × WSE model. One event generates 8–12 pieces of content. The highest-return activity in our entire plan.

💰
Paid Ads

Facebook, Instagram, Google, TikTok. Ads amplify the content we've already made — they never replace real content.

🎭
Real Student Stories

Named alumni sharing specific results. "Got a promotion 6 months after WSE." Specific, named, real — AI and humans both trust these.

📧
Follow-Up Emails

A series of 3–5 helpful emails after someone shows interest. Content-focused, not pushy. Links back to our articles.

🧪
Free Tools

Free English Level Assessment. Easy, no-commitment entry point. Shows WSE quality before asking anyone to pay.

Action
Every campaign: activate at least 2 influencers who publish written content + run ads + offer a free entry point (assessment or guide). If there's an event, the influencer must attend and publish their review within 48 hours.
4
Step 4 of 4 · Long-Term Growth

X — eXpansion: Build Something That Grows Itself

This is the long game. The first three steps run every campaign. This step is what happens when you do those three steps consistently for 6–12 months — the results start to build on themselves. More content means more AI visibility. More alumni means more referrals. More referrals means lower cost to get new students.

🏆
Happy Students Refer Friends

Build an alumni network with events and perks. Word-of-mouth is still the most trusted form of marketing — and it costs nothing.

📚
Content That Keeps Working

A 100-article library doesn't stop working when the campaign ends. Each article keeps bringing people to WSE 24 hours a day.

🔁
Annual Research Report

"WSE English Impact Report — Indonesia 2026." Publish once a year. Become the go-to source AI cites for English learning data in Indonesia.

🌐
WSE as a Recognized Name Online

Wikipedia page, Google Knowledge Panel, consistent presence across platforms. Makes AI recognise WSE as a verified, established brand.

🤝
Ongoing Partnerships

Formal agreements with Glints, SWA, Kompas for regular co-created content. Each partnership creates content at no additional production cost.

📈
Monthly Health Check

Every month: check if WSE appears in AI search for 20+ target questions. Every quarter: publish a new data report. Never go 30 days without new published content.

Action
Ongoing: treat every piece of content as a long-term investment, not a one-time campaign. Link every new article to existing ones. Check AI appearances monthly. The brands that win AI search are the ones that never stop publishing.

The APEX Framework didn't come from guesswork. Every part of it is based on well-known books and studies used by marketing teams at major companies around the world. Here is where each piece comes from.

🏛️

Aaker Brand Identity Model

David Aaker · "Building Strong Brands" (1996)
UC Berkeley Haas Business School
Known as "The Father of Modern Branding"

"A brand is more than a logo or a product. It is a set of associations and promises — and building those consistently is what creates lasting business value."

This gives us Step A — the idea that before we do anything, we must be crystal clear about what WSE stands for. Aaker's model is how we define WSE's identity, personality, and promise.
Brand Identity Brand Promise Brand Personality
🔺

Keller Brand Pyramid (CBBE)

Kevin Lane Keller · "Strategic Brand Management" (1998)
Dartmouth Tuck School of Business
Used in 200+ business schools globally

"Building a strong brand means guiding customers through a journey — from simply knowing who you are, all the way to becoming someone who genuinely loves and recommends your brand."

This gives us Steps P, E, and X — the four-level journey from awareness to loyalty. It also explains why our influencer strategy must create emotional connection, not just visibility.
Awareness → Loyalty Customer Journey Brand Resonance
⚖️

PESO Model®

Gini Dietrich · "Spin Sucks" (2014)
Certified at Syracuse University
Used by Fortune 500 companies worldwide

"Paid, Earned, Shared, and Owned media are not separate strategies. They are one connected system — and brands that use all four together build far more trust than those who only run ads."

This gives us our channel structure — the reason we use our own website, media coverage, paid ads, AND influencers together. Each one does something the others cannot.
Paid Ads Media Coverage Influencers Own Content
🤖

GEO — Generative Engine Optimization

Princeton University · Published at ACM KDD 2024
The world's first peer-reviewed study on AI search visibility
Aggarwal, Murahari, Rajpurohit et al.

"Content that includes real data, clear structure, and reliable sources is 40% more likely to be cited by AI tools than content that does not — regardless of which company wrote it."

This is the newest and most important piece — it explains exactly why we write content a certain way, why we need original data, and why media coverage matters so much for AI visibility.
AI Search Visibility Content Structure Data-First Writing
